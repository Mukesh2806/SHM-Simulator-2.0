Physics Behind Simple Harmonic Motion

Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) is a special type of periodic motion where the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement and acts in the opposite direction. A mass attached to a spring is a classic example.

Key Concepts and Equations:

  • *Restoring Force (Hooke's Law):* $F = -kx$, where $k$ is the spring constant and $x$ is the displacement from equilibrium.
  • *Angular Frequency ($\omega$):* $\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$, where $m$ is the mass.
  • *Period ($T$):* The time for one complete oscillation. $T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$.
  • *Displacement ($x(t)$):* If starting from maximum positive displacement at $t=0$, $x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)$, where $A$ is the amplitude.
  • *Amplitude ($A$):* The maximum displacement from the equilibrium position.

In this simulation, we consider an ideal spring and negligible friction.
